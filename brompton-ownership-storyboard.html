Andy's Ownership Journey

1
Delivery
🏠 Andy's flat, London E1 — Tuesday afternoon
Andy's phone buzzes. A notification slides down.
Andy (to himself)
"Finally. It's here."
He opens the front door. The box is there. He smiles.
2
Registration
📱 That evening — Andy downloads the Brompton app
He logs in and is prompted to register his bike. He finds the serial number on the frame.
Andy (to himself)
"Purchased at Balfes Bikes. Sales associate: Sally." That's a nice touch.
The bike feels real now.
Customer Retailer Brompton 👤 Andy is identified from day one — before he's ridden a mile 🏪 Balfes gets credit for the sale, even post-purchase 📊 Brompton owns the direct relationship from first open
3
Onboarding — Three Questions
📱 Same evening — the app asks Andy three things
How often do you ride? How do you plan to use your Brompton? What's your postcode?
Andy (to Ella)
"It's asking for my postcode. Clever — it'll know my routes."
The next day, the app surfaces Andy's first piece of content: the Ready to Ride checklist.
Andy (to himself)
"Recommended routes. Cycling in traffic. What to wear. OK — I'm actually going to do this."
Over the next few evenings, he works through it. He's preparing. He's excited.
Customer Retailer Brompton 👤 Confidence built before the first commute 📊 Segmentation data captured — Andy's content feed is now personalised
4
The First Commute
🌅 Early morning — Andy's front door, E1
Andy stands on the street with the T Line. He unfolds it for the first time. His hands are slightly clumsy. It clicks into place.
Andy (to himself)
"Right. Here we go."
He rides to work. It takes longer than the tube. He arrives in a better mood than he has in years.
His phone buzzes at his desk.
Andy
"Verified Owner badge. 'Welcome to the fold.'" He screenshots it and sends it to Ella.
Customer Retailer Brompton 👤 Identity moment — Andy is now a Brompton rider, not just a buyer 📍 First ride data captured; usage patterns begin to build
5
Early Milestones — 25 & 50 Miles
📱 A week and a half later — Andy hits 25 miles
A video appears in the app. A personal message from their CEO.
Andy (to himself)
"This doesn't feel like marketing. He's just — talking to me." He watches it twice.
Andy hits 50 miles. The app delivers a reward: one free coffee at WatchHouse.
Andy (texts Ella)
"Riding to WatchHouse after work. Treat from Brompton."
Ella
"Only if you bring one home."
Friday afternoon. He rides to WatchHouse near the river. Folds the bike beside him. Sits outside with his coffee watching the city. It's a good moment.
Customer Retailer Brompton ☕ Tangible reward at a moment of high engagement 🤝 WatchHouse partnership delivers lifestyle alignment, not just discount
6
100 Miles — The Friend Pass
📱 Andy hits 100 miles
The app unlocks a 3-Day Friend Pass — a Brompton hire voucher to send to anyone.
Andy (texts Max)
"Got you something. Three days on a Brompton, on me. Well — on them."
Max
"Is this real? I've been going on about these things for months."
A week or so later. Andy's phone lights up.
Andy (calls Ella)
"Max got one. They're inviting us to the factory."
Ella
"Us? The factory? In Greenford?"
Andy
"Just like Willy Wonka, right?" He laughs.
Customer Retailer Brompton 🎁 Referral feels like generosity, not a programme 📈 Max is a new sale triggered by Andy — CAC near zero 🏭 Factory tour deepens both Andy's and Max's brand commitment
7
Discovery — City Guides
🗺 Saturday morning — Andy opens the app
Brompton's city guides: crowdsourced routes, Brompton-friendly cafes, pubs and museums that welcome the bike inside.
Andy (to himself)
"East London Loop. Eight miles. Starts Wapping, ends at The Mayflower." I didn't know half these places existed.
He follows it alone. It's one of the better Saturdays he's had in a while.
Customer Retailer Brompton 🗺 The bike reshapes how Andy experiences the city 📍 Route data and engagement signals build the ride profile
8
The Flat Tyre — Phil
🛞 Midway through the ride home — a quiet backstreet, Shoreditch
Andy gets a flat. He's crouching over the wheel with no idea what to do. A passing rider slows, doubles back, and stops.
Phil
"Need a hand?"
Andy
"I've got no idea what I'm doing, honestly."
Phil uses his own spare tube and toolkit to fix it. They chat for ten minutes. Phil is a member of the London Brompton Club.
Andy
"Do you always carry a spare tube?"
Phil
"Always. We look after each other."
Phil waves it off and rides on.
9
Skills & Confidence Building
📲 Back home — Andy opens the Brompton Academy
He finds "How to Fix a Flat." He watches it but isn't confident he could do it alone.
Andy (to himself)
"That looks manageable." He checks the class booking — £10, one hour at Balfes. "Ten quid. I'll make that back the first time I'm not stranded halfway to work."
Andy arrives at Balfes. Sally is there. Tom the mechanic leads an hour with Andy and six other owners.
Tom · Balfes Mechanic
"By the end of this hour you'll be able to fix a flat on any street in London."
Free spare tube with any toolkit purchase. Andy buys the toolkit, plus a second tube. Just in case.
Two days later, riding home, Andy spots another Brompton owner crouched over a flat on the pavement.
Andy (to the stranger)
"I've got a spare tube. Here — let me show you."
He's surprised by how good it feels. Phil was right.
Customer Retailer Brompton 🔧 Andy gains confidence and belonging simultaneously 🏪 Balfes drives a toolkit sale and a service booking — the app sent the customer back 🔄 Community behaviour self-replicates without Brompton orchestrating it
10
A surprise & a reminder
📱 Andy hits 250 miles — the app sends stickers
A set of reflective customisation stickers arrives in the post. Andy lets his daughter decide where they go.
Stacey
"That one. And that one. And definitely that one."
Customer Retailer Brompton 👤 Andy has fun sharing his new bike with his daughter. 🎁 The surprise gift is a goodwill deposit. Andy's personalised bike supports a closer identity with the brand.
📱 Andy hits 500 miles
The app sends a service reminder: new tyres, brake pads, a full tune-up. Members can access a loaner bike while the bike's in the shop.
Andy (to Ella)
"They'll lend me a bike while mine's in the shop."
Ella
"Book it."
Customer Retailer Brompton 👤 Loaner bike removes the only barrier to booking 🔧 Proactive service booking drives Balfes revenue 📊 Content feed evolution mirrors Andy's growing rider identity
11
The World Championships
🏆 Brompton World Championships — Andy goes alone, on a whim
The event is chaotic and joyful — fancy dress, fold-off competitions, riders from all over the world.
Andy (to Mila, Paris Brompton Club)
"This is nothing like I expected."
Mila · Paris Brompton Club
"It never is. What city are you from?"
Andy
"London."
Mila
"Does everyone in London wear such funny costumes when they ride?"
Mila introduces him to someone she's just met. Andy does a double take. It's Phil — the man from the Shoreditch flat. Phil recognises him too. They laugh.
Phil
"Have you fixed your own flat yet?"
Andy
"Someone else's in fact!"
Customer Retailer Brompton 🌍 Community feeling made tangible — strangers become friends ❤️ The event is the product. No ad spend could replicate this
12
The Club — Ella Rides
🚴 Phil invites Andy to join a London Brompton Club ride
Andy
"I've got plans with my wife that weekend."
Phil
"Bring her — Brompton club members get free bike hire passes. Get her a bike."
Andy hires a Brompton for Ella through the app. They join Phil's ride on a Sunday morning — around twenty riders, a mix of ages and abilities.
Ella (mid-ride)
"This is actually really good."
Andy
"It's cool, right?"
They ride through the park. Stop for coffee. Come home two hours later still talking about it.
Ella (walking in the door)
"I want one."
Andy (smiling)
"I know."
Customer Retailer Brompton 👫 The product extends into Andy's closest relationships 🏪 Hire drives a purchase; another Balfes customer in the making 📈 Ella is the next sale — no campaign required
13
1,000 Miles and Beyond
🌀 Andy crosses 1,000 miles
The app marks it with a personalised summary — commutes completed, weekend routes, friends referred. Ella's Brompton arrives the same week.
Andy (to himself)
"214 commutes. I haven't taken the tube in six weeks."
The app is now surfacing multi-day routes, cycling holidays, international riding trips. Andy opens the Paris city guide and bookmarks it.
🌀 Andy crosses 2,000 miles
The app puts it in context: The distance from London to Istanbul, with Paris in the middle.
Andy (to Ella)
"2,000 miles. The app says that's London to Istanbul. Paris is halfway."
Ella
"Right, that's it. We're going to Paris. Spring. I've been waiting for you to hit this number."
Andy
"Mila — from the Paris Brompton Club — she's on the app as a Local Host. She offered to show us the city properly."
Ella
"We should bring Stacey. We can hire a Brompton for her — she'll absolutely love it."
Andy
"A family trip to Paris on Bromptons. I genuinely didn't see my life going this way."
From the other room —
Stacey
"Are we going to Paris?! Does the Eiffel Tower have a bike rack?"
Customer Retailer Brompton 🌍 Brompton has restructured Andy's life — not just his commute. He's healthier, happier, and lives Life Unfolded. 🏪 Membership actively drives engagement & revenue back to the store. Win-win incentives support trust and engagement on the sales floor. 🔄 The flywheel: one sale in-store became a family, a community, and a trip abroad. Andy is on his way to being a strong advocate for the brand.
