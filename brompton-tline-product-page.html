Questions from the community
112 questions answered by Brompton owners and staff
Q
Is the 12-speed worth the premium over the 6-speed T Line for mostly flat city riding?
Community answer (47 people agree): For a genuinely flat city like Amsterdam, the 6-speed is probably sufficient. But if you have any hills, bridges, or headwinds, the extra range is transformative — especially the lower gears. The steps between gears are much smaller too, so you're always in the right gear. I regret not getting the 12-speed first time around.
Asked by Owen M. ✓ 47 found this helpful
Q
How does it actually compare to a full-size road bike for longer rides (30+ miles)?
Verified owner reply: I regularly do 40-mile weekend rides on mine — through the Peak District, not just flat roads. The geometry takes 20 minutes to adapt to, and then you forget you're on a folding bike. The 12-speed gearing handles the climbs, the Schwalbe ONE tyres are fast, and the carbon bits absorb road buzz surprisingly well. It is not a road bike, but it's genuinely fun for long days out.
Answered by Alex K. · Verified T Line owner ✓ 89 found this helpful
Q
How durable is the Blasted Titanium finish? Does it scratch easily?
Brompton staff: The Blasted Titanium is a raw titanium finish — naturally resistant to corrosion and doesn't require paint protection. Surface marks from normal use tend to blend into the matte texture over time, giving it character rather than looking damaged. Owners often say it improves with age. For maximum protection, the Ceratech Black finish adds a ceramic coating, but Blasted Titanium is extremely durable in everyday use.
